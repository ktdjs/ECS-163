How many blood pathways are circadian?

All 5 pathways shown here are time-dependent — each was selected because it has documented ~24 h oscillation in human whole blood. Across the broader transcriptome, meta-analyses place the fraction of rhythmically expressed blood genes at roughly 10–15% under normal conditions (Möller-Levet et al. 2013; Wittenbrink et al. 2018). Sleep deprivation or shift work can suppress these rhythms — flatter amplitudes and higher inter-donor variance — consistent with sleep-regulation studies such as Möller-Levet et al. (2013).

The Circadian Core Clock is the driver: BMAL1/CLOCK transcribe PER/CRY repressors on a ~24 h feedback loop, and their output gates the other four pathways — OxPhos via NAD⁺/SIRT1, IFN-α via IRF3/STING sensitivity, HSP-stress via cortisol rhythm, and neutrophil trafficking via CXCL5 oscillation.
